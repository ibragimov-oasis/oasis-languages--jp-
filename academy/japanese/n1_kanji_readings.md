# JLPT N1 Kanji Readings

## Introduction

JLPT N1 requires knowledge of approximately 2,000+ kanji characters, representing near-native literacy. This level includes archaic kanji, scientific terminology, literary expressions, and rare readings found in classical texts, specialized fields, and advanced literature.

## Study Tips for N1 Kanji

1. **Read extensively in Japanese**: Novels, newspapers, academic papers, technical documents.
2. **Study kanji etymology**: Understanding origins and historical changes helps retention.
3. **Learn specialized vocabulary**: Focus on fields relevant to your interests or work.
4. **Master ateji and special readings**: These appear frequently in native materials.
5. **Study classical texts**: Exposure to literary Japanese deepens understanding.
6. **Use professional materials**: Business documents, legal texts, scientific papers.
7. **Practice handwriting**: Helps distinguish similar kanji and reinforces memory.
8. **Learn kanji families**: Group kanji by radicals and phonetic components.
9. **Understand context**: Same kanji can have vastly different meanings in different contexts.
10. **Think in Japanese**: At N1 level, direct thinking in Japanese is essential.
