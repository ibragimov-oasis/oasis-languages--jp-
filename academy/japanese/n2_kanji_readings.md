# JLPT N2 Kanji Readings

## Introduction

JLPT N2 requires knowledge of approximately 1,000 kanji characters. This level includes rare readings, special readings (特別な読み方), literary kanji, and complex compounds used in formal writing, news, and professional contexts.

### Compound Kanji with Changed Pronunciations

**Rendaku (連濁) - Sequential voicing:**
- 神 (かみ) + 様 (さま) = 神様 (かみさま)
- 本 (ほん) + 箱 (はこ) = 本箱 (ほんばこ)
- 手 (て) + 紙 (かみ) = 手紙 (てがみ)

**Sokuonka (促音化) - Gemination:**
- 一 (いち) + 回 (かい) = 一回 (いっかい)
- 学 (がく) + 校 (こう) = 学校 (がっこう)
- 日 (にち) + 記 (き) = 日記 (にっき)

**Onbin (音便) - Sound euphony:**
- 手 (て) + 伝う (つたう) = 手伝う (てつだう) [irregular]
- 時 (とき) + 々 = 時々 (ときどき)

## Study Tips for N2 Kanji

1. **Master special readings**: Many N2 kanji have irregular readings in compounds.
2. **Learn four-character idioms**: Essential for formal writing and speaking.
3. **Study compound patterns**: Understand how kanji combine to create new meanings.
4. **Read extensively**: Newspapers, novels, essays - expose yourself to varied contexts.
5. **Learn kanji etymology**: Understanding origins helps remember meanings and readings.
6. **Practice handwriting**: Reinforces memory and helps with similar-looking kanji.
7. **Use context clues**: Same kanji can have different meanings based on context.
8. **Study systematically**: Group by radicals, meanings, or readings for better retention.
