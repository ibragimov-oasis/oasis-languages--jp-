# JLPT N3 Kanji Readings

## Introduction

JLPT N3 requires knowledge of approximately 650 kanji characters. This level introduces complex compound words with mixed on'yomi and kun'yomi readings, specialized vocabulary, and kanji used in formal and business contexts.

## Key Features of N3 Kanji

### Mixed Readings in Compounds

Many N3 words combine on'yomi and kun'yomi:
- 手紙 (てがみ) - letter: kun + on
- 物語 (ものがたり) - story: kun + kun
- 場所 (ばしょ) - place: kun + on
- 読み物 (よみもの) - reading material: kun + kun

### Sound Changes (連濁 - Rendaku)

When kanji combine, sounds often change:
- 学 (がく) + 校 (こう) = 学校 (がっこう)
- 本 (ほん) + 屋 (や) = 本屋 (ほんや)
- 人 (ひと) + 々 = 人々 (ひとびと)

### Transitive/Intransitive Pairs

N3 introduces many verb pairs:
- 増える (increase - intrans) / 増やす (increase - trans)
- 混ざる (get mixed) / 混ぜる (mix)
- 続く (continue) / 続ける (continue something)

---

## Study Tips for N3 Kanji

1. **Learn compound patterns**: Study how kanji combine to form new meanings.
2. **Master reading variations**: Pay attention to irregular readings.
3. **Study transitive/intransitive pairs**: Essential for natural Japanese.
4. **Read authentic materials**: Newspapers, blogs, novels at your level.
5. **Focus on context**: Same kanji can have different meanings in different contexts.
6. **Learn collocations**: Some kanji always appear with certain other kanji.
7. **Practice writing**: Helps reinforce memory and recognition.
8. **Use frequency lists**: Focus on the most common N3 kanji first.
